How LLMs work

Understand the mechanics behind large language models — from tokenization to prediction.

Watch how AI reads your text — step by step
step 1 of 4
What just happened?
1
Breaking into Tokens
Your sentence is split into small word-pieces called tokens. "ChatGPT" might become two tokens: "Chat" + "GPT". Each token gets a unique ID number so the computer can work with it.
2
Words → Numbers (Embeddings)
Each token ID is looked up in a giant table to get a list of numbers (called a vector). These numbers capture the word's meaning — "king" and "queen" will have similar vectors.
3
Finding Connections (Attention)
The model figures out which words are related to each other. In "The fox jumped", "fox" and "jumped" are strongly linked. The brighter the cell in the grid, the stronger the connection between those two words.
4
Guessing the Next Word
Using everything it learned, the model scores every word in its vocabulary and picks the most likely next word. It then appends that word and repeats — that's how it writes a full response to your prompt!
